TAPTURE

Jotting down ideas quickly, when we don’t have that piece of paper

With Tapture we are focusing on the experience of taking notes with mobile devices, which would be a substitute of analog note taking measure such as a pen and a piece of paper. We learned that many people prefer using pens and paper than using the apps on their phones because pens and paper is quick and easy. And our secondary research and competitive analysis also show that the existing note-taking apps in the market are complicated and less intuitive to use, and these are the main factors which stop the users from using it as the primary tool.

This project was selected as one of the 6 finalists for the 2015 Shobe Startup Prize competition from HCDE Department at University of Washington.

Research

To investigate our design topic, we first conducted a few interviews with our potential users who take digital notes frequently with their mobile devices, in order to gain more insights of the existing note taking products.

We learned that typing on the screen is difficult, people write short notes in one line and tend to make a bulletin list; voice memo is difficult for review and not searchable; steps to initiate the note taking app may take away the original intentions.
